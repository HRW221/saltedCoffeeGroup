Wildflower Meadows

The Exeter campuses boast a diverse array of plants collected from around the globe, alongside native species that support local flora and fauna. Grounds staff have identified and established areas conducive to the growth of native plants, including perennial and annual wildflower meadows. These meadows, strategically placed alongside watercourses or deliberately established, enhance biodiversity by creating nectar highways and offering habitats for various wildlife. Careful management is essential to prevent grasses and weeds from overpowering the wildflowers.

Current sites hosting these features include Laver/Harrison, Streatham Court, Forum South Piazza ponds, and others. Species such as narcissus, poppy, and orchid attract a range of wildlife, enriching the campus environment. Additionally, a chamomile lawn has been created at Lafrowda steps. Together, these plants contribute to 21 different habitats, supporting diverse insect, amphibian, bird, and mammal populations, facilitating numerous student and research projects while enhancing the overall biodiversity of Exeter and the South West region.
